# Titanic Survival Analysis (EDA)

This project focuses on an independent Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the historical Titanic passenger dataset to investigate the underlying demographic and socio-economic factors that determined passenger survival.

🔗 **Live Notebook:** [View my original work on Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/code/harrisawan/titanic-eda-survival-analysis)

## 📊 Key Findings
* **Baseline Survival:** The overall survival rate across the 891 analyzed passenger records was **38.38%**, meaning the vast majority did not survive.
* **Gender Disparity:** Gender proved to be the strongest single predictor of survival. Women survived at a rate of **74.2%**, compared to just **18.9%** for men, verifying the historical "women and children first" protocol.
* **Socio-Economic Class Impact:** Passenger ticket class played a critical role in survival probabilities:
  * 1st Class: **62.9%** survival rate
  * 2nd Class: **47.3%** survival rate
  * 3rd Class: **24.2%** survival rate
* **The Intersectional Story:** Combining demographics revealed extreme disparities: 1st-class female passengers achieved a **96.8%** survival rate, while 3rd-class male passengers survived at a mere **13.5%**.
* **Age Factor:** The average age of survivors was 28.3 years versus 30.6 years for non-survivors, showing a marginal survival advantage for younger individuals.
